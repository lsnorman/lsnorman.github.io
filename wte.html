What to Expect

Your First Visit

Your First Visit

Visiting us for the first time? Here's a step-by-step guide on what to expect:

  • Initial Discussion: Your chiropractor will start with a conversation about your health history, including surgeries, accidents, lifestyle factors, and other details affecting your current health.
  • Thorough Assessment: Each patient's case is unique. Your chiropractor will conduct a comprehensive history and examination to create a personalized treatment plan tailored to your needs.
  • Physical Examination: During this examination, your chiropractor will focus on your neuromusculoskeletal system. For your comfort, consider wearing loose, comfortable clothing. If you prefer, you can have a family member or third person present during the treatment.
  • Diagnostic Referrals: If necessary, further studies such as blood tests, x-rays, or MRI can be referred.
  • Treatment Explanation: Before proceeding, you'll be provided a clear explanation of the proposed treatment, potential complications, and you'll have the opportunity to ask any questions.
  • First Treatment: The initial consultation often includes the first treatment session if deemed appropriate.

Treatment Techniques

Treatment Techniques

Chiropractic care utilizes a range of techniques including:

  • Manual therapy
  • Manipulation (adjustments)
  • Mobilisation
  • Exercise rehabilitation
  • Soft tissue techniques
  • Medical acupuncture
  • Ultrasound

Treatment Duration

Treatment Duration

The treatment length varies. Typically, 2 to 6 treatments are common, with sessions gradually spaced out. Longer-standing or recurrent problems might need extended treatment. Other therapies might be suggested to enhance the outcome.

Post-Treatment

Post-Treatment

  • Aftercare Advice: Supportive advice and home exercises are provided post-treatment.
  • Maintenance Treatments: Periodic treatments are available for long-term wellness, often shorter in duration.
  • Recovery Guidance: Temporary symptoms like soreness may occur post-treatment. Your chiropractor will guide you through the recovery process.

How to Find Us
