About Me

My introduction to Chiropractic was as a 17-year-old patient and this is where my interest in health and well-being started.

I have a passion for working with people to increase their understanding of pain - research shows that increased pain literacy leads to better self-management. My role is to enable people to access the knowledge they need to take charge of their health and well-being. I enjoy using a wide range of techniques including adjustments (the chiropractic word for joint manipulation), soft tissue work, mobilisation alongside specific exercises, with a particular interest in Pilates, relaxation-strategies for managing stress and western medical acupuncture. I am particularly interested in helping people to become aware of the impact that physical, emotional and lifestyle stressors can have on the body and explore ways to minimise these.

I graduated from Macquarie University, Australia in 1995 with a Masters in Chiropractic. I am registered with the General Chiropractic Council (GCC) and hold membership with the British Chiropractic Association, the Royal College of Chiropractors (RCC), the British Medical Acupuncture Society (BMAS) and the British School of Meditation (BSoM).

My love of learning and education led me to a Senior Lecturer (Clinical) post at the University of Surrey on their Chiropractic Programme. I subsequently obtained my PGCE at the Institute of Education, London. I am part of the Test of Competence Assessor team with the GCC. I am a meditation teacher and I am currently working towards a Diploma in Pain Management with the RCC, a Certificate of Western Medical Acupuncture and have recently embarked on study and training to become a psychotherapist.

As part of my commitment to excellence in care I make sure that I engage in joyful, playful activities like: learning new things, country walking, swimming in the sea, sitting in the hammock in my garden, meditation, cycling, upholstery, learning Japanese, travelling and being with my family and friends.

The Clinic

Collins Chiropractic is dedicated to providing you with the best quality care in order to enhance the health of each individual in an ethical, efficient and caring way.

Rebekah is committed to ongoing professional development in order to facilitate the provision of up-to-date chiropractic care for each person. She aims to work alongside patients to provide education, treatment and steps towards their personal goals. Rebekah happily co-manages care with other doctors and health practitioners and will refer for imaging if needed. People of all ages are welcome into the clinic where the environment is calm and relaxing.

Rebekah is a highly qualified healthcare professional who has undertaken a 5-year degree and is registered with the General Chiropractic Council (GCC), the UK’s regulatory body for chiropractic. Rebekah also works at Wickford Chiropractic Clinic, Essex and belongs to the following associations and organisations:

British Chiropractic Association (BCA)

Royal College of Chiropractors (RCC) member

British Medical Acupuncture Society (BMAS)

British School of Meditation (BSoM)

The clinic is situated in the Health Club at Prested Hall. There are personal trainers in the gym, Real Tennis coaches, swim school, Pilates (reformer) instructor, beautician, massage therapist, a café onsite and lots of parking available. Use of the gym and pool require membership.
